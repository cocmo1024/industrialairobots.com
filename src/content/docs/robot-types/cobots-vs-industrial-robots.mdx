# Cobots vs Industrial Robots

This comparison is often framed too simplistically. Collaboration is not the same thing as "no guarding," and industrial robots are not automatically a poor fit for flexible operations. The correct choice depends on task intensity, space, pace, tooling, and how the cell will actually be run.

## Cobots are strongest when

- Payload and speed requirements are moderate.
- Flexibility and operator interaction matter more than maximum throughput.
- The cell benefits from easier redeployment or lighter integration overhead.

## Industrial robots are strongest when

- Throughput, payload, reach, or duty cycle demands are high.
- The cell already justifies structured guarding and dedicated space.
- Operational robustness matters more than perceived collaboration simplicity.
